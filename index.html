Hello, World!

Welcome to my personal corner of the internet, where I share a few parts of my life: some of my research, creative pursuits, adventures, and the faith that guides me. I’ve found it difficult to place these parts into separate spaces, since none of them really captures who I am as a whole. While many people prefer to keep professional and personal life distinct, for me they often feel connected. In the end, the work we do is shaped, in small ways, by the kind of person we are becoming.
By training, I am a computational biologist and bioinformatician interested in immuno-oncology, currently pursuing a PhD in the Department of Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University. Outside of research, I help with the Events Committee at the Graduate Christian Fellowship at JHU and serve on the worship team at Emmaus Church. I also enjoy working with my hands: making fiber arts (including my ANTIBUDDY crochet pattern), cooking, and occasionally tinkering with my bike.
I care about education and hope, in time, to share what I’ve learned with students in different places around the world as a travelling academic. So far, life has taken me to four countries: Indonesia (18 years), Australia (2 years), China (1.5 years), and the United States (3.5 years and counting). I hope the journey continues!
